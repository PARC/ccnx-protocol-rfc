CCNx

This is the home page for the CCNx specifications. These are driven by PARC and maintained by the IRTF ICN Research Group (ICNRG) and other organizations at large. The world is invited to participate in progressing, implementing, and verifying these specifications.

What is CCNx?

Content-Centric Networking, or CCN, is a request and response communication protocol whereby consumers of data fetch chunks of information from producers using a name. Each chunk can be individually authenticated and opportunistically cached in the network. The vision of CCNx is to develop, promote, and evaluate a practical instantiaton of the CCN protocol.

See the ICNRG IRTF group for more information about this field as a whole.
