CCNx

This is the home page for CCNx, an Information Centric Networking (ICN) protocol driven by PARC and advanced by the IRTF ICN Research Group and other institutions at large.

What is CCNx?

The vision of CCNx is to develop, promote, and evaluate a new approach to a communication architecture called content-centric networking. We seek to carry out this mission by creating and publishing open protocol specifications and a software reference implementation of those protocols. We provide support for a community of people interested in experimentation, research, and commercialization of this technology.

See the ICNRG IRTF group for more information about this field as a whole.

Specifications

The CCNx protocol is detailed in the following specification documents:

  • CCNx Messages in TLV Format
  • CCNx Semantics
  • CCNx URI Scheme

Participation

You can participate by joining the ICNRG and CCNx mailing lists and attending any of the meetings. The CCNx protocol is driven by the Palo Alto Research Center (PARC) and is based upon the PARC Content-Centric Networking (CCN) architecture, which is the focus of a major, long-term research and development program. There are interesting problems in many areas still to be solved to fully realize and apply the vision, but we believe that enough of an architectural foundation is in place to enable significant experiments to begin. Since this new approach to networking can be deployed through middleware software communicating in an overlay on existing networks, it is possible to start applying it now to solve communication problems in new ways. CCNx is an invitation to join us and participate in this exploration of the frontier of content networking.
